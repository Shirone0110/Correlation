Correlation vs Causation

What is correlation used for?

Correlation is used to describe a linear relationship between different variables. Correlation can show how strong the relationship between the variables is.

What is the difference between correlation and causation?

Correlation describes relationship between different variables. Variables with strong correlation does not mean one variable is the cause of the other.
Causation shows that the change of one variable is the reason for the change of another variable. Causation relationship will results in strong correlation, but strong correlation does not mean causation relationship.
